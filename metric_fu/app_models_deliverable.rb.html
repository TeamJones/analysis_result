1# Deliverable is a zip or a file that the students submit for a course
  
2#
  
3# There are two ways for a student team to upload their deliverables.
  
4#
  
5# Way 1) On the curriculum pages for each task,  under the "Submitting Your Work" tab, there is a
  
6# "submit your deliverable link" -- this gives the student the ability to name their deliverable, and upload a
  
7# single attachment (i.e. one doc, ppt, or zip file). By default a Course Number and the Assignments are  provided for
  
8# the student. Student needs to choose which asignment he/she is going to submit. The deliverable is individual or team
  
 9# deliverable will be checked on the basis of the assignment he/she is submitting. If a faculty member tries to do this,
  
10# they won't see any courses lists since they aren't taking the course as a student.
  
11# Students can submit only team deliverable and one individual deliverable per task, although they can update the
  
12# attachment as many times as they like.
  
13#
  
14# Every member of a team can see team deliverables, only the individual can see their individual deliverable.
  
15#
  
16# Way 2) A student team can upload their deliverable by clicking on "My Deliverables" on the left hand navigation and
  
17# select "New" at the bottom of the page. This is a little less efficient because the student needs to select a
  
18# course and task number. It is a quick way for a student to see everything they have submitted for their courses.
  
19#
  
20# If a student or team accidentally uploads the wrong file, they can upload additional files. All files are
  
21# kept in the system, but the convention is that the faculty will only examine the last uploaded file.
  
22#
  
23# The faculty assigned to that team will receive an email whenever a student submits a deliverable. The faculty
  
24# can click on the email and provide either written comments in an text area or provide a file attachment back.
  
25# If you annotate a word document you would upload the word document.
  
26# If you annotate multiple files then you would attach a zip, just like the students do.
  
27#
  
28# If the faculty needs to see who has provided feedback or if they loose the email sent by the system, there is a f
  
29# faculty interface for examining the deliverables. It is not perfect, it was developed by the students and they
  
30# didn't have a strong sense of how we would use it. It currently shows all the deliverables submitted
  
31# for a course sorted by recent changes first. There is no way to filter by faculty member, or sort by task,
  
32# or sort by team, or sort by whether feedback has been provided. Control F in the browser is a life saver.
  
33# It does show you which deliverables have been graded. In theory, just relying on the emails should be sufficient.
